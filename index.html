What role do the costs of implementing and maintaining advanced soccer technologies play in creating a competitive gap between wealthy and less-resourced leagues?

Soccer is rapidly advancing. New technolgies break through each day making the performance and accuracy of the game even more precise. However, with new technology comes an underlying downside. Soccer is played all over the world, making it hard to fund the game in places where people might be less fortunate.

VAR

In soccer, VAR stands for video assistant referee. Using VAR, referees in a control room analyze footage from the game in order to assist the on field referee in making the right call. This could be a goal, penalty, fouls, etc.

Training

New sports analytics keep track of match data. This includes pocession percentages by each team, shots on target, completed dribbles, etc. Using this data coaches are able to cater to their teams needs and help focus on lacking components of their team.

Performance

A growing popular technological advancement in soccer has been GPS vests. Players are able to wear these vests which analyze the players heart rate, distance, acceleration, and top speed.
